Random random

A collection of probability/combinatorics questions I like.

Combinatorics for shifts of finite type. An ever-evolving writeup of combinatorial and probability questions for pattern-avoiding words, aka shifts of finite type.

Combinatorial stochastic processes. An ever-evolving catalog of probability models and questions, including particle processes, diffusions, martingales, and random number theory.

Sum constructions. Questions related to sumsets of real numbers.

Collisions among random walks. A survey I wrote as a graduate student about collision times among independent random walkers. Contains a brief literature review, plus some original computations for simple random walks on the integers and a finite cycle.

Statistics for coupon collection. Consider an iid sequence of random variables drawn from some probability measure on the integers. What can we say about the set of values collected up to some time? The theory of large deviations describes the empirical measure, and thus can describe the 'coupon set,' up to some error, so more delicate methods are necessary. This note computes, for a certain class of light tailed measures, the limiting distribution of a certain statistic defined over the 'coupon set.'
