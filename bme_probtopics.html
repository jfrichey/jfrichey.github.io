Syllabus

This course will focus on three models: random walks, percolation, and first passage percolation.
For each topic, we will start by reviewing classical results and methods, and then move on to recent ideas and papers.

Papers for presentation

Here are the papers I suggest for presentations. Your presentation should consist roughly of two parts: describing the general structure of the paper or or of one of its theorems (~5-10 minutes), and a deep dive into a proof of one lemma or fact (~20-25 minutes).

Simple Random Walk

  • LLN for the size of the range of 2D simple random walk: Dvoretzky and Erdos, or Jain and Pruitt , or Jiao (this is very similar to sections 2/3 of Dvoretzky & Erdos), or Erdos and Taylor
  • Local times for 2D random walk: Cerny
  • Coupling of two SRWs so they don't intersect: Benjamini and Kozma
  • Exponents for cut times of simple random walk: Lawler
  • Capacity of the range of random walk on \(\mathbb{Z}^4\): Asselah, Schapira and Sousi

Percolation

  • Percolation on \(\mathbb{Z}^2\) with random diagonal edges: Rolla
  • Uniqueness of the infinite cluster: Burton and Keane
  • Sharpness of phase transition: Duminil-Copin and Tassion
  • Isoperimetric profile: Pete

First Passage Percolation

  • High dimensional FPP: Auffinger and Tang (2016), and a recent update to that paper Auffinger and Tang (2025)
  • Possible Asymptotic Shapes: Haggstrom, Meester
  • At least two geodesics: Hoffman (2004)
  • Limit shape is not always a polygon: Damron and Hochman (2010)
